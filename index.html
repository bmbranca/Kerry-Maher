I am what I is

When I was a young actor in North Carolina, driving hours to audition for a two-line role in some made-for-TV cheesy drama, I dreamed of success complete with money, fame, prestige and clout. Eventually, I thought to myself, directors will be begging me to accept roles that I would smugly reject as unworthy of an actor of my caliber. But at one of these endless auditions, I overheard a veteran character actor offer some sage advice to a young actor who was arrogantly verbalizing what I had merely contemplated. After kindly asking the young man to calm down, he simply told him to accept the fact that, “we am what we is.” While the grammar is atrocious, is the philosophy is profound and describes perfectly where I find myself in my acting career.

In the United States I had moderate success as an actor. There were a couple of years where I was able to survive solely from acting. I acted in probably a hundred commercials, several TV series and a few movies. The rest of the time I taught at universities and community colleges where an exceptional audition might result in a cancelled class or two. Now, I am retired, living in South Korea and surviving cancer. And I gleefully accept that “I am what I is.” I’m a 67 year-old, bald, chubby character actor who is extraordinarily happy to not only be acting again, but to be alive.

So, if you have a role for someone of my girth, appearance and age, I’d love the opportunity to audition for you. Know in advance that you will encounter an actor with passion, experience and dedication. “I am what I is.”
